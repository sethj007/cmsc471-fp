THE STORY

2010 — H3N2 Re-emerges

After the 2009 H1N1 pandemic, H3N2 quietly reasserted itself as the dominant seasonal strain. Surveillance networks began tracking its spread with renewed urgency.

2014 — The Vaccine Mismatch

A major antigenic drift caused the flu vaccine to be poorly matched to circulating strains. Case counts surged across North America and Asia.

2017 — One of the Worst Seasons in Decades

Another mismatch year. One of the lowest vaccine effectiveness rates ever recorded. Hospitalizations spiked in the US, Australia, and Europe.

2020 — COVID Nearly Eliminated the Flu

H3N2 almost disappeared. Pandemic measures decimated influenza transmission globally. Cases dropped to historic lows across most of the world.

2024 — The Rebound

H3N2 rebounded as pandemic measures lifted. Surveillance networks now track thousands of sequences per year to stay ahead of the next mismatch.
